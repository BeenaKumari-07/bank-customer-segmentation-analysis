# 🏦 Bank Customer Segmentation & Transaction Analytics

## 📌 Project Overview
This project involves a comprehensive analysis of a retail bank’s dataset, containing 880,000 customers and 1,000,000 transactions. The goal was to move beyond basic reporting and perform Value-Based Segmentation to identify high-net-worth individuals, demographic trends, and seasonal transaction patterns.

Total Portfolio Value: $119.82bn Total Transaction Volume: $1.63bn

## 🛠️ Tech Stack & Skills
Data Visualization: Power BI 

Data Processing: Power Query / Excel (Cleaning & Transformation)

Analytical Techniques: * RFM Logic (Recency, Frequency, Monetary)

Pareto Analysis (80/20 Rule)

Cohort Analysis (Age & Gender Demographics)

Trend Analysis (Time-series transaction flows)

## 🔍 Key Technical Insights
### 1. Value-Based Segmentation (The Pareto Effect)
I categorized the customer base into four distinct tiers: Low, Medium, High, and Premium.

The Insight: The "Premium" segment represents only ~11% (101K) of the customer base but holds $91.76bn (76.5%) of the total balance.

Action: Recommended a "High-Touch" concierge service for this segment to ensure 0% churn.

### 2. Demographic "Gold Mines"
Age Analysis: While the 36-50 age group is the largest (0.54M), the 18-25 age group holds the highest average balance ($0.58M).

Strategic Takeaway: This suggests a segment of "High-Earning Young Professionals" or inherited wealth that requires specialized long-term investment products.

### 3. Transactional Velocity
Volume vs. Value: The Low-Value segment accounts for the highest transaction frequency (398K transactions).

The Insight: These customers are the "daily users" of the bank. Improving their mobile app experience is key to maintaining operational scale.

## 📈 Strategic Recommendations
Bridge the Gender Gap: With a 71% male-skewed database, there is a massive opportunity to launch "Women in Finance" initiatives to capture the remaining 29% of the market.

Seasonal Re-engagement: Data showed a significant drop in activity toward October. Automated re-engagement campaigns (emails/app notifications) should be triggered in September to prevent this slump.

VIP Retention: The top 10 customers contribute a disproportionate amount of transaction volume. These individuals should be assigned dedicated relationship managers.
